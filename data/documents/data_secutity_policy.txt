ACME CORPORATION - DATA SECURITY AND PRIVACY POLICY
Version 4.2 | Information Security Team | 2024
=====================================================

1. DATA CLASSIFICATION
-----------------------
ACME classifies data into four levels:

PUBLIC: Information approved for public release
        Examples: Marketing materials, press releases

INTERNAL: General business information for employees only
          Examples: Internal memos, meeting notes, org charts

CONFIDENTIAL: Sensitive business information with restricted access
              Examples: Financial reports, customer data, contracts

RESTRICTED: Highly sensitive data requiring strictest controls
            Examples: Employee salaries, trade secrets, legal documents

2. DATA HANDLING REQUIREMENTS
-------------------------------
Public Data:      No special handling required
Internal Data:    Must not be shared outside organization
Confidential:     Encrypted storage, need-to-know access only
Restricted:       Encrypted, access logged, manager approval required

3. ACCEPTABLE USE POLICY
--------------------------
Company systems must only be used for legitimate business purposes.
Employees must not access, modify, or delete data without authorization.
Personal use of company devices should be minimal and not interfere with work.
Downloading company data to personal devices is strictly prohibited.
Using personal cloud storage (Google Drive, Dropbox) for company data is not allowed.

4. DATA ENCRYPTION REQUIREMENTS
---------------------------------
All laptops and mobile devices must have full disk encryption enabled.
Confidential and Restricted data must be encrypted in transit and at rest.
Use only company-approved encryption tools.
Email encryption is required for sending Confidential or Restricted data.
USB drives must be encrypted — unencrypted USB drives are not permitted.

5. ACCESS CONTROL POLICY
--------------------------
Principle of Least Privilege: Employees get minimum access needed for their role.
Access requests must be submitted via IT Portal with manager approval.
Access must be revoked immediately upon employee termination.
Quarterly access reviews are conducted by department heads.
Shared accounts and shared passwords are strictly prohibited.

6. INCIDENT REPORTING
----------------------
Any suspected data breach must be reported within 1 hour of discovery.
Report to: security@acmecorp.com and your direct manager
Do not attempt to investigate or fix a breach yourself.
The Security team will conduct a formal investigation.
Employees who fail to report incidents may face disciplinary action.

7. REMOTE WORK SECURITY
------------------------
Always use VPN when working remotely.
Do not work on confidential data in public places like cafes.
Lock your screen when stepping away from your workstation.
Use company-provided devices for work — personal devices need MDM enrollment.
Ensure home WiFi is password protected with WPA2 or higher.

8. THIRD PARTY AND VENDOR SECURITY
------------------------------------
Vendors must sign a Non-Disclosure Agreement before accessing company data.
Third-party access is logged and monitored.
Vendors must comply with ACME data security standards.
Vendor access is reviewed and renewed annually.
Any vendor data breach must be reported to ACME security team immediately.

9. COMPLIANCE AND REGULATIONS
-------------------------------
ACME Corporation complies with:
- GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation)
- CCPA (California Consumer Privacy Act)
- ISO 27001 Information Security Standard
- SOC 2 Type II Compliance

10. PENALTIES FOR VIOLATION
-----------------------------
Minor violations: Written warning
Moderate violations: Suspension without pay
Severe violations: Termination and potential legal action
Data theft: Criminal prosecution
